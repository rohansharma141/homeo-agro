Pest and Insect Control in Crops — Preventive and Corrective Approach

Pests, insects and plant-related viral issues can affect crop growth and final output. A preventive approach is always better than waiting for serious crop damage.

Prevent first, react second

Virosil is an agro-purpose pesticide product developed for all types of pests, viruses and insects. It can be used as a preventive support product and also where pest issues are already present.

Waiting for visible damage often means the problem is bigger than what's on the surface. Preventive applications are usually less costly and more effective.

Typical application schedule

The product can be applied at the time of sowing, after 20–25 days of sowing, and again after around 30 days for pest control. This pattern gives the crop preventive coverage through its most vulnerable early stages.

In case of active pest presence, the label recommends using it continuously for 3 days or as required. This corrective approach is intended for situations where pest issues have already become visible and need intervention.

A balanced approach

Farmers should always follow dose instructions and avoid overuse. More product is not necessarily more effective — and overuse can have its own consequences for soil, water and adjacent crops. Stick to the recommended 2 ml per 1 litre of water unless an agriculture expert specifically advises otherwise.
