How to Use Plant Growth Support Products at the Right Crop Stage

The timing of application is very important in farming. Different products are useful at different stages of crop growth, and a product applied at the wrong moment may not deliver the benefit it's designed for.

A simple stage-by-stage view

At the time of seed sowing, products such as Plantowin-D can be used. This sets the soil up for the early growth phase of the crop.

After 20–25 days, when the plant starts developing and the first watering is given, crop nutrition support products may be applied — Uri Fast for grains and pulses, or Plantowin-K for fruits, vegetables and flowers.

Around 40 days after sowing, a second application can support further growth. This typically coincides with the second watering schedule.

The right product at the right stage isn't about doing more — it's about doing the right thing at the right moment.

At the final plant stage, when buds or beans appear, Last Tuch may be used as a spray to support plant stimulation, fruits and better growth. This is a finishing-stage product, not a growth-stage product.

The principle

Using the right product at the right time helps farmers support plant health more effectively. It also helps reduce wastage — products applied outside their useful window may not give the intended results, even at the correct dose.
